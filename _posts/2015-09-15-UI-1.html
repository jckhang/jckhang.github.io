"It’s an age-old NYC problem. It’s some time after 4PM and you step out to look for a cab. You giggle as
you watch a tourist repeatedly try and hail cabs with the off-duty lights on, but then you realize the joke
is on you too. Most of the cabs have off-duty lights on. Better luck next time." --Ben Wellington, I Quant NY

1 Problem addressed

Ben Wellington tries to using taxi data to prove this myth1 and finding out how to fix this problem.

2 Analytics Used

Ben looked at the data from 2013 collected by Chris Wang2.

Ben ploted the NYC Taxi Fare Revue per minute and confirmed a 3 hours(2:50pm to 4:30pm) reduced service. See Figure 1.

To answer why taxi drivers choose the busiest time of the day to do a shift change, Ben plotted the fare revenue for the previous and next 12 hours at each time of day. Based on base fare, the optimal time (intersect point on the plot) is 5:08AM/PM. See Figure 2.

The main solution Ben gave was to move the curve leftward with a reverse surcharge and the new shift hour will be at about 3PM instead of 5PM. Win/Win to passengers and cab drivers. See Figure 3.

3 Limitations and Scalability

Ben's Model is just simple as it seems. It's easy to duplicate. But the data was outdated, and as Uber cars' addition to the market, their impact on the hourly revenue can not be neglected. The new data and new model should be tested.

4 Impact of the Application

The TLC has released the data of 2014 and 2015 Yellow and Green Taxi Trip data early at August. This should credit to the work that Ben insist in 'Open Data Movement'.
